Vikas Khanna

Vikas Khanna is an Indian-American Michelin-starred chef, restaurateur, and cookbook writer. Born in Amritsar, India, he was inspired to cook by his grandmother, who taught him the art of blending spices and herbs. At the age of 17, he moved to the United States and started his culinary journey in New York City. He has since become one of the most recognized chefs in the world, having cooked for dignitaries such as Barack Obama and the Dalai Lama. Khanna has also authored several books, including "Flavors First" and "My Great India Cookbook," and has been a judge on the popular TV show "MasterChef India."
